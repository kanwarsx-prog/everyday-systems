# Everyday Systems Website Improvements

## Objective

Make the website support Sushil's job search by presenting genuine, credible experience rather than sounding like a consultancy sales pitch.

The site should feel like:

> A senior enterprise platform and transformation leader with real delivery experience across Salesforce, ServiceNow, AI-enabled products and financial services.

It should not feel like:

> A digital agency selling generic AI transformation services.

---

## Overall Positioning

### Current issue

Some of the language sounds polished but too sales-led, especially on the homepage and services pages. It risks making the work feel less personal and less genuine.

Examples to soften:

- Architecting AI-Native Enterprise Ecosystems
- £100M+ value delivered
- Bridging the gap between Legacy IT and Intelligent Automation
- Deliver certainty in uncertain times
- Unprecedented efficiency
- New frontier
- Platform overhaul

### Recommended positioning

Use this as the core message across the website:

> I am a senior enterprise platform and transformation leader with real delivery experience across Salesforce, ServiceNow, AI-enabled products and financial services. I bridge strategy, architecture and delivery — and I have actually done the work.

## Projects Section

### Current issue

The Projects section is valuable, but some pages currently sound like fully commercial products rather than personal builds, prototypes or experiments.

That may create doubt if the projects are not live, funded or externally used products.

### Rename the section

Replace:

```text
Projects
```

With:

```text
Personal Builds & Experiments
```

Alternative:

```text
Product Experiments
```

### Add status labels to every project

Each project should clearly show its maturity level.

Use one of:

```text
Status: Concept
Status: Prototype
Status: Live build
Status: In progress
Status: Personal experiment
```

### Add a short explanation block to every project

Use this format:

```text
Status: Prototype

Built to explore:
AI workflows, product thinking, data models and user experience.

My role:
Product strategy, architecture, design and build.
```

### Why this matters

This makes the work feel honest and credible. Recruiters will respect curiosity and hands-on initiative, but they may be suspicious if personal projects read like fully launched SaaS businesses.

### Suggested project intro

```text
These are personal builds and experiments I have used to explore product ideas, AI workflows, user experience, data models and platform thinking.

Some are prototypes, some are concepts, and some are live builds. They are included here to show how I think, design and build — not to present them as fully commercial products.
```
